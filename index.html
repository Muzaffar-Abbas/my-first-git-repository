Overview Gilgit is the capital and largest city of Gilgit-Baltistan, a semi-autonomous region in northern Pakistan, bordering China, Afghanistan, and India. Nestled at the confluence of the Karakoram, Himalaya, and Hindu Kush ranges, it offers dramatic landscapes, including towering peaks like K2 and Nanga Parbat, fertile valleys, and glacial lakes. The region spans roughly 72,496 km² and is home to about 1.7 million people, representing diverse ethnic groups such as Shins, Yashkuns, Balti, Burusho, and Wakhi. Wikipedia

Importance of Gilgit

; Gilgit is a major tourist destination in Pakistan, serving as a hub for trekking and mountaineering expeditions in the Karakoram mountain range of northern Pakistan. +2 Tourism and Adventure Gilgit is a hub for trekking, mountaineering, and adventure sports. Visitors can enjoy gentle valley treks for beginners or high-altitude expeditions for experienced climbers. Adventure activities include white-water rafting, paragliding, and mountain biking. The Karakoram Highway, the world’s highest paved road, connects Gilgit to other regions and provides access to scenic spots like Hunza Valley, Skardu, and Phander Valley. Springtime brings cherry and apricot blossoms, creating picturesque landscapes.
